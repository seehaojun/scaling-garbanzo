Section 01

What Is Vibe Coding?

Vibe coding is building software by describing what you want in plain English — and letting an AI turn your words into a working app. You do not write code. You describe the purpose, the users, and what it should do. The AI handles everything else.

Think of it like briefing a contractor. You describe the renovation you want. They handle the blueprints and construction. You review, give feedback, and refine. The key skill is being clear about what you need — not knowing how to build it.

This is not a toy or a shortcut for simple tasks. It is a genuine shift in who can build functional digital tools. Policy officers, analysts, and subject-matter experts can now prototype solutions in hours, not months — without waiting in an IT queue.

Section 02

From Idea to App in 5 Steps

The process is straightforward. The most important step happens before you open any tool.

1

Clarify Your Idea

Before touching any tool, answer three questions in plain language: What problem does this solve? Who will use it? What must it do? Keep it to the core — ignore everything else for now.

2

Choose Your Tool

Pick an AI-powered app builder. For most government or professional use cases, start with Claude (for complex logic and document work) or Lovable (for fast full-web app prototyping with a database).

3

Write Your First Prompt

Describe what you want. Be specific about the purpose, 3–5 key features, the type of user, and any data it needs to handle. The AI generates a working prototype. It will not be perfect — that is expected and fine.

"Build a simple web app for a small government team to track parliamentary questions. Each entry should have: question text, assigned officer, due date, status (Pending / In Progress / Done), and a notes field. Show overdue items prominently on the dashboard."
4

Review and Refine

Treat the first output like a rough draft. Give feedback in plain English: "Move the due date column earlier." "Add a filter by officer." "Use a lighter colour scheme." Most simple tools are usable within 3–10 rounds of feedback.

5

Test, Share, Deploy

Test with a small group of actual users. Gather real feedback. Most tools offer one-click publishing to a live web link. Continue iterating based on how people actually use it.

Finance & Budget

"We manage public money. We need clarity, speed, and accountability."

Finance teams spend significant time on manual tracking, budget reporting, and chasing updates across divisions. Vibe coding can replace messy spreadsheets and email chains with simple, purpose-built tools that give everyone a live view of the same data.

You do not need a new enterprise system. You need a lightweight tool that does exactly what your team does — nothing more.

Budget Variance Tracker ▶
A simple dashboard where each division enters monthly actuals against their approved budget. The tool flags variances above a set threshold, sends automatic reminders, and generates a one-page summary report ready for the CFO. No Excel. No email chains. All divisions see the same numbers in real time.
Grant Application Screener ▶
Upload a batch of grant applications as PDFs. The AI reads each one, extracts key fields (organisation name, amount requested, purpose, eligibility criteria met), and produces a structured shortlist with a compliance check — flagging applications that are missing documentation or fall outside the scheme criteria.
Internal Procurement Q&A Bot ▶
Train a chatbot on your procurement rulebook, GeBIZ guidelines, and internal SOPs. Staff can ask questions like "What is the threshold for a waiver of tender?" or "Which form do I use for IT purchases above $50,000?" and get instant, accurate answers — reducing reliance on your procurement team for routine queries.

Water & Environment

"We protect long-term resources. We deal in complex data and long time horizons."

Environmental agencies handle large volumes of sensor data, field reports, and public feedback. The challenge is rarely collecting data — it is making sense of it quickly and communicating findings to non-technical stakeholders. Vibe coding can bridge that gap.

A tool that turns raw readings into a clear weekly briefing, or lets field officers log incidents from a mobile form, can save dozens of hours a month and improve response times.

Reservoir Level Dashboard ▶
A live visualisation tool that pulls in water level readings from multiple reservoirs and displays them in a simple, colour-coded dashboard. Senior officers get an instant picture of system status without needing to read raw data files. Alerts are triggered when levels fall below or rise above defined thresholds.
Field Incident Reporting App ▶
A mobile-friendly form for field officers to log environmental incidents (illegal dumping, water quality anomalies, drainage blockages) on the spot. Submissions are automatically tagged by location and type, routed to the right team, and tracked to resolution — replacing paper forms and WhatsApp messages.
Public Feedback Summariser ▶
Upload a batch of public feedback submissions from a consultation exercise. The AI reads and categorises each one by theme (e.g. flooding concerns, recreational access, water pricing), identifies the most common issues raised, and produces a structured summary with representative quotes — in minutes, not days.

Infrastructure & Planning

"We build for decades. We manage hundreds of moving parts at once."

Infrastructure agencies coordinate contractors, timelines, compliance checks, and public communications across dozens of concurrent projects. The administrative overhead is significant. Vibe coding can automate the routine — status updates, document checks, stakeholder notifications — so your team focuses on what requires human judgement.

Project Status Hub ▶
A central tracker where project managers update milestones, flag delays, and log contractor updates. The tool auto-generates a weekly summary report for leadership showing which projects are on track, at risk, or overdue — and what actions are pending. Replaces the manual PowerPoint update cycle.
Document Compliance Checker ▶
Upload contractor submissions (method statements, safety plans, insurance certificates). The tool checks each document against a checklist of required sections and criteria, flags missing or non-compliant items, and produces a structured review note — cutting days off the administrative review cycle.
Resident Feedback Triage Tool ▶
During major construction projects, public feedback volumes spike. This tool ingests feedback from multiple channels, classifies each item (noise complaint, access issue, safety concern, general enquiry), assigns priority, and routes to the relevant team — with a draft acknowledgement response ready to send.
🎓

Education

"We shape people. We manage complexity at national scale — one school at a time."

Education agencies operate across thousands of schools, teachers, students, and parents. Much of the administrative burden falls on teachers and school leaders — time that should go to students. Vibe coding can give schools small, targeted tools that solve their specific problems without waiting for a ministry-wide system rollout.

It is also a model for what we want students themselves to learn: that technology is a tool you shape, not a system that shapes you.

Teacher Admin Reducer ▶
A simple tool where teachers describe their class, topic, and learning objectives — and the AI generates a structured lesson plan with differentiation suggestions, discussion questions, and assessment ideas. Not to replace teacher judgement, but to turn a 45-minute planning task into a 5-minute review-and-edit. Time saved goes back to students.
School Feedback Analyser ▶
Upload end-of-term student or parent feedback surveys. The tool reads all responses, identifies recurring themes, flags urgent concerns, and produces a structured summary for the principal — distinguishing between praise, operational feedback, and policy-level concerns. A school with 300 survey responses can have a clear picture in under 10 minutes.
CCA Portfolio Tracker ▶
A student-facing tool for logging CCA participation, leadership roles, and reflections over their school years. At the end of each year, it auto-generates a structured portfolio summary for MOE or post-secondary applications — saving teachers and students the scramble of reconstructing records at graduation.

Health

"We protect lives. We operate under pressure, with high stakes and scarce attention."

Health agencies and institutions generate enormous volumes of data, reports, and correspondence. The challenge is speed and accuracy — getting the right information to the right person at the right moment. Vibe coding can build targeted tools for specific workflows without requiring a full system overhaul.

Policy Brief Generator ▶
Upload a stack of research papers, epidemiological reports, or international health agency publications. The tool extracts key findings, synthesises the evidence, and produces a structured policy brief in the format your ministry uses — with a recommendations section ready for human review and sign-off.
Outbreak Monitoring Dashboard ▶
A live situation report tool that aggregates case counts, cluster locations, and vaccination rates from uploaded data files. Produces a daily one-page visual briefing for senior leadership — with trend lines, geographic heat maps, and a plain-language summary of what the numbers mean. Replaces a manual half-day collation process.
Staff Query Assistant ▶
Train a chatbot on your institution's HR policies, clinical SOPs, and operational guidelines. Staff can ask questions about leave entitlements, procurement procedures, or clinical protocols and get immediate, accurate answers — freeing HR and admin teams from routine queries and reducing the risk of staff acting on outdated information.
Section 04

What Vibe Coding Is and Is Not For

Used well, vibe coding accelerates teams and puts capability in the hands of domain experts. Used carelessly, it creates tools that look functional but fail under real-world conditions. Know the boundary.

✓ Well suited for

  • Internal workflow and tracking tools
  • Data dashboards and visualisations
  • Document analysis and summarisation
  • Simple forms and submission portals
  • Prototypes to test ideas before investing in full builds
  • Tools used by small, known teams

⚠ Proceed with caution or escalate

  • Systems handling sensitive personal data (PDPA considerations)
  • Mission-critical platforms requiring security certification
  • Deep integrations with national databases
  • Public-facing services at scale
  • Replacing professional engineering for complex platforms
The core principle for leaders: Your job is not to code. Your job is to think clearly about the problem. The clearer your brief, the better the output. The skill that matters most is the same one good policymakers already have — the ability to define a problem precisely and communicate it well. Vibe coding hands that skill a new kind of leverage.
